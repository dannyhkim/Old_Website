Visual Arts Projects

My Hanbok

Pencil + Charcoal

Hanbok is traditional korean clothing worn during special occassions. This was my first attempt at realism using willow charcoal and charcoal pencil. However, I was pleasantly surprised at the results and was very satisfied that I drew this photo as it was a very special piece of clothing I wore during my childhood.

Two-Point Perspective Hallway

Pencil, Fineliner + Thin Brush

One of my visual arts classes taught me how to do two-point perspective drawings. In this work, I used fineliner to draw the lines, and then used a slightly wet fine brush to bleed them. This added more volume and shading to the walls, making the image look more visually interesting.

Aboriginal Bike Rack Design | Adobe Illustrator + InDesign

In this project, I developed a bike rack design influenced by Aboriginal art. The goal was to create something creative but functional that could potentially be implemented in areas of Downtown Toronto. The board on the left informs readers of the entire process that occurred in order to create the final mock-up. A rationale was needed in order to understand the purpose of this project. I then proceeded to conduct research on Aboriginal art in order to influence the appearance of my preliminary sketches. After I came up with a final design, I rendered it in Adobe Illustrator and created a mock-up of its potential use.
